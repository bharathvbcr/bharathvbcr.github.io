Textured Insoles Impact on Performance

Project Overview

This groundbreaking research project investigated the effects of textured insoles on postural control, locomotor adaptation, and cognitive performance during dynamic activities. As the lead researcher at the adidas-Arizona State University Center for Engagement Science, I spearheaded a multidisciplinary team to explore the potential benefits of innovative insole designs for diverse populations.

Addressing the Problem

Recognizing the limitations of traditional cognitive tests during dynamic activities, I identified a critical gap in our understanding of how sensory feedback from the feet impacts overall performance. This led to the central question: Can strategically textured insoles enhance balance, proprioception, and cognitive function during movement?

Innovative Ideas and Methodologies

To tackle this complex issue, I developed a suite of novel testing protocols:

  1. Visual Analog Scale Test (VAST): Quantified subjective perceptions of insole texture
  2. Balance and Stability Test (BAST): Measured mediolateral sway during static balance tasks
  3. Dynamic Adjusted Lane Test (DALT): Assessed gait adaptability in confined spaces
  4. Dynamic Auditory Stroop Test (DAST): Evaluated cognitive performance during movement

These custom-designed tests allowed us to gather comprehensive data on the interplay between foot sensory input and various performance metrics.

Procedure and User Interaction

  1. Participant Recruitment: Carefully screened and selected 15 male participants (age 23.3 ¬± 2.67) using the Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire (PARQ).
  2. Informed Consent: Developed a clear, thorough consent process to ensure participants fully understood the study's scope and potential risks.
  3. Testing Sessions: Conducted rigorous 75-minute sessions, guiding participants through each protocol with precision and care.
  4. Insole Treatments: Tested four insole conditions - three prototypes (P1, P2, P3) and a control (smooth sole).
  5. Data Collection: Utilized cutting-edge sensors and recording devices to capture high-quality data across all test modalities.
  6. Feedback Loop: Regularly solicited participant feedback to refine our protocols and ensure comfort throughout the testing process.

Key Achievements and Findings

  1. Data Analysis: Employed advanced statistical techniques to uncover significant trends:
    • Up to 34% reduction in mediolateral sway with textured insoles
    • 6% improvement in gait adaptability
    • Insoles I2 and I3 demonstrated the lowest average response times in cognitive tasks
  2. Visualization: Created compelling data visualizations to effectively communicate our findings to both scientific and general audiences.
  3. Threshold Effect Discovery: Identified a critical "sweet spot" in insole texture that maximizes performance benefits.
  4. Response Accuracy: Achieved close to 95% accuracy across all insole conditions in cognitive tasks.

Impact and Future Directions

This research has significant implications for multiple populations:

  • Athletes: Potential for enhanced proprioception and reduced injury risk
  • Aging Individuals: Improved balance and stability for fall prevention
  • Neuromotor Deficiencies: Possible therapeutic applications to improve gait and cognitive function

Our findings lay the groundwork for revolutionizing insole design, opening new avenues for personalized footwear that can dramatically improve performance and quality of life.
